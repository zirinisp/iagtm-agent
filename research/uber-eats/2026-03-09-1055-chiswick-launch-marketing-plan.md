## Recommended Campaign Structure

### Philosophy: Combined Ads + Offers from Day One

Instead of following the old pattern (ads in Ads Manager, offers in Uber Eats Manager, no connection), Chiswick should be IAGTM's first location to run **combined campaigns** in Ads Manager where ads and offers are paired in a single campaign with unified targeting, budget, and reporting.

### Phase 1: Launch Blitz (Days 1–30) — Maximum Exposure

The priority is **visibility + conversion**. Every person in the Chiswick delivery radius needs to see IAGTM and have a reason to order.

### Phase 2: Build Momentum (Days 31–60) — Targeted Growth

By day 30, you'll have data on which campaigns convert. Shift from broad to targeted.

#### Adjustments
1. **Review ROAS** — If any campaign is below 500% ROAS (the "New location" benchmark), pause or reallocate
2. **Switch Sponsored Listing to goal-based bidding** — Now you have enough conversion data for the algorithm to optimize
3. **Add keyword campaigns for Chiswick-specific terms** — "chiswick delivery", "chiswick greek food", "w4 food delivery"
4. **Launch Happy Hour offer (14:00–17:00)** — Chiswick opens at 16:00, so a "pre-dinner" Happy Hour with 20% off could fill the early slot

#### New Campaign: Chiswick Happy Hour (Combined)

### Phase 3: Retention + Loyalty (Days 61–90) — Lock In Before Commission Rises

The 10% commission window closes around 5 June 2026. Before that, build a base of repeat customers who will keep ordering even at 25% commission.

#### New Campaigns
1. **Lapsed Customer Win-Back**: £5 Off min £25 — target anyone who ordered in weeks 1–4 but hasn't returned
2. **Uber One Loyalty**: Free Soft Drink >£15 — reward early adopters who are Uber One members
3. **Custom Creatives**: Upload branded images showcasing the BOGO deal and Chiswick-specific messaging

#### Phase 3 Budget: ~£475/week (~£1,900/month)

## Competitive Edge: What Makes This Different

| Old Approach (Other Locations) | New Approach (Chiswick) |
|-------------------------------|------------------------|
| Ads in Ads Manager, offers in Eats Manager | Combined campaigns in Ads Manager |
| No technical link between ad and offer | Unified targeting, budget, and reporting |
| AM creates offers on our behalf | Self-serve offer creation |
| Per-location offers only | Brand-level targeting (reach new-to-brand across all 6 locations) |
| No Happy Hour, no Discount Items | Use new offer types (Feb 2026 features) |
| Blanket offer targeting | Customer behavior targeting on offers (lapsed vs returning) |

Chiswick becomes the **proof of concept** for the combined approach. If it works (and the data suggests it will — 42% sales lift from BOGO + 15% from Sponsored Listings = compounding effect), roll it out to all locations in Phase 3 of the main migration roadmap.

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Low order volume in first weeks | Normal for new locations — Peckham benchmarks suggest 580–733% ROAS is acceptable |
| Rating drops below 4.5 | Operational excellence is critical — focus on prep times, accuracy, packaging |
| BOGO co-funding not applied | Confirm in writing with AM before launching; check Ads Manager co-funding column |
| Offer duplication (AM creates in Eats Manager too) | Clear communication: Chiswick = Ads Manager only |
| Commission jumps to 25% at day 91 | Phase 3 focuses on retention so customers are habitual before rates increase |
| Opening hours limit (16:00 daily) | Happy Hour won't work until hours extend; adjust Phase 2 timing if hours don't change |
