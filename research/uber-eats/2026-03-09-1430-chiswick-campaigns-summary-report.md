## Platform Findings

### Ads and Offers Are Separate Campaign Types

From the **[Sponsored Listings FAQ](https://help.uber.com/en/merchants-and-restaurants/article/sponsored-listings-faq?nodeId=ad985cec-625e-4951-afac-18926088be6a)**:

> *"At this time it is not possible to create a single campaign to manage sponsored listings and offers together."*
>
> *"These tools are complementary and serve different objectives. You can run campaigns for both simultaneously."*

Uber Ads Manager has two distinct sections:
- **Ads** → Sponsored Listings, Keyword Targeting (visibility — pay per click)
- **Offers** → Discounts, Free Delivery, Free Item, Happy Hour (conversion — pay per redemption + £0.58/order)

### The £0.58/Order Fee — Open Question

Every offer created in Ads Manager shows **£0.58/order** on the campaign page. This fee does **not** exist for offers created in Uber Eats Manager.

| Platform | Offer Cost | Additional Fee |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Uber Eats Manager** | Discount amount only | **£0.00** per order |
| **Ads Manager** | Discount amount only | **£0.58** per redeemed order |

**What we don't know yet:**
1. Does the £0.58 include a built-in Sponsored Listing (i.e. automatic ad placement)? If so, the offers may already have visibility without needing separate ad campaigns.
2. Or is it purely a platform fee for using Ads Manager's self-serve offer creation?

**How we'll find out**: Run the offers for a few days and check whether the campaign reporting shows ad-related metrics (impressions, clicks, CTR). If it does, the £0.58 covers a Sponsored Listing. If it only shows offer redemptions, it's just a platform fee.

### Multiple Offers Don't Conflict

From the **[UK Running Offers Guide](https://www.uber.com/en-GB/blog/running-offers-on-uber-eats/)**:

> *"You can combine any offers that are on the item level with any offer on the basket level, running more than one offer simultaneously."*

| Our Offer | Level | Stacks With |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| 25% Off min £20 | Basket-level | Item-level offers (Free Side Salad, BOGO) |
| Free Delivery | Basket-level | Item-level offers |
| Free Side Salad >£30 | Item-level | Either basket-level offer above |
| BOGO Chicken Wrap (via AM) | Item-level | Either basket-level offer above |

A customer sees one basket offer (25% off OR Free Delivery — Uber picks the best) plus any applicable item offers (Free Side, BOGO) on top. New customers see the 25% off; returning customers see Free Delivery.

### Offers Created in Ads Manager Are Fully Locked Once Active

Once published, **nothing can be edited** — name, dates, budget, targeting, all locked. The only option is "End" (terminate) or "View" (read-only). To change anything, you must end the campaign and create a new one.
